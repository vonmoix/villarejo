## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and executive headhunters evaluating Manuel Villarejo as a C-level/Director IT candidate. They arrive via LinkedIn or a direct referral, spend 60–90 seconds scanning, and decide whether to reach out. They're experienced, fast readers — they skim for signal, not decoration.

### Brand Personality
Authoritative · Precise · Warm
The interface should feel like a well-tailored suit: understated, impeccably constructed, nothing out of place. No noise, no clutter — just clarity and weight.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme (professionals reviewing CVs often do so in office/evening contexts). Warm amber/gold accent already established. Editorial direction — closer to a high-end architecture firm's website than a tech startup's. Avoid anything that screams "I used a template." The palette and fonts (Bricolage Grotesque + Hanken Grotesk + Fira Code) are locked and working well.

### Design Principles
1. **Signal over noise** — every element must earn its place. If it doesn't help a recruiter make a decision, remove it.
2. **Confident hierarchy** — headings, sections, and key credentials must be scannable in 10 seconds. No flat typography.
3. **Tactile precision** — details matter: spacing rhythm, border weights, hover states. Sloppiness at the micro level undermines executive credibility.
4. **Warmth through restraint** — amber accent used sparingly creates warmth without feeling loud. More accent = less impact.
5. **Zero AI tells** — no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no left-border stripes on cards, no identical card grid layouts.
