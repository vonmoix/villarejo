03 — Overall Impression

Gut reaction: what works, what doesn't, and the single biggest opportunity.

What works: v2's bottom half is now substantially stronger than v1. The section order Skills → TechStack → Certifications → About → Contact creates a coherent capability arc that builds from "what I do" to "what I know" to "who I am" to "let's talk." The video in About breaks the text-density rhythm at exactly the right moment — a visual breath before the close. The consulting-focused Contact copy is specific and confident.

What doesn't (resolved): The About bridge text is now typographically elevated — text-ink font-medium at 1.0625rem distinguishes the personal statement from body copy. The resume download button is wired; activation requires dropping the PDF into /public/. No unresolved design issues remain.

Where to go next: The only ceiling-raiser left is content — quantified outcomes in experience entries ("Led €Y budget", "Reduced MTTR by X%") would move the portfolio from impressive credentials to compelling case. That's beyond design scope.

08 — What's Working

What v2 does well — both inherited strengths and new additions.

Video in About

A muted loop landscape video is a rare design choice in professional portfolios — most opt for photography or skip visuals entirely. The cinematic 21:8 crop on desktop reads as intentional art direction. VP9 primary source (277KB) with h264 fallback and JPEG poster ensure fast loading across all browsers.

Skills Grid Balance

6 groups in a 2×3 layout closes symmetrically on all breakpoints. The addition of "Cloud Migration & Modernisation" is not padding — it's a genuine capability cluster that was previously absent. The grid now reads as a complete taxonomy, not an incomplete list.

Capability Cluster Flow

Skills → TechStack → Certifications is the correct narrative order: what I do → what I use → how I prove it. This three-section cluster forms a coherent credential arc that was fragmented in v1. The reader never has to wonder why Tech Stack appears between sections with unrelated content.

Consulting Contact Copy

The v2 Contact section names specific engagement types (M&A integration, ERP/HRIS consolidation, AI-powered operations) and adds a geography signal (Europe · APAC · UK) and a response expectation (24h). This is the difference between a passive "reach out" and an active consulting brief.

Animation Grammar (inherited)

Every motion follows the same enter-from-below (y: 14 → 0, opacity: 0 → 1) pattern with expoOut easing. v2 components use identical Framer Motion config. The consistency means new sections feel native, not grafted on. useReducedMotion is respected throughout.

Accessibility Foundation (improved)

v2 adds aria-label and role="img" to all 3 inline SVG path icons in TechStack (AWS, Azure, Microsoft 365). Combined with the existing skip link, ARIA mobile menu, focus trap, and prefers-reduced-motion support, v2 has a stronger accessibility baseline than v1.

10 — Questions to Consider

Provocative questions that might unlock better solutions.

The About bridge text says "I bring the same curiosity to AI-powered operations and continuous learning as I do to enterprise transformation." What specifically about Manuel's curiosity? One concrete example — a tool built, a workflow automated, an experiment run — would make this sentence land rather than float. What would that example be?
The video in About is the only moving element below the hero. Does it earn its visual weight at the 21:8 cinematic crop on desktop? Or would a 16:9 crop with object-position tuned to a specific focal point better serve the content? The aspect ratio is an art direction decision, not a default — it deserves scrutiny.
Emerging Technologies is the last of 6 skill groups, but it contains the most forward-looking claims (Agentic AI, Claude Code, AIOps). What if it were the first group? Leading with "where I'm going" rather than "where I've been" changes the entire strategic positioning of the Skills section — and signals future-forward to the CTO persona who skims first groups only.
The consulting Contact copy names three equally-weighted engagement types. Which one generates the most interesting conversations? Which one is rarest in the market? That's the one that should lead — and the others should follow or be cut entirely.
